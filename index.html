ADA LOVELACE

THE FIRST COMPUTER PROGRAMMER

Dec. 10, 1815 - Nov. 27, 1852

Early Life

1815- Ada was born to poet Lord Byron and Annabella Byron. Her father left shortly after her birth and she never knew him personally.


She became educated through tutors and the first mathematics professor at the University of London - Augustus De Morgan.


1835- She married William King, and when he was created an earl in 1838, she became countess of Lovelace.


Career

1833- Ada was introduced to Charles Babbage and became interested in his machines- particularly the Analytical Machine.


She was able to see the potential in computers beyond their computational purpose. Ada was able to predict their usage in art and music.


1842- Ada published a note describing an algorithm for the Analytical Machine to compute Bernoulli numbers. It is considered to the be first published algorithm.


“That brain of mine is something more than merely mortal.”

- Ada Lovelace

Legacy

1852- Ada died at the age of 36 from uterine cancer.


1980- The early programming language Ada was named for her.


2009- The second Tuesday in October has become Ada Lovelace Day, on which the contributions of women to STEM fields are honored.
